MDGAN


Computer-aided diagnosis systems with deep learning frameworks have been used to identify benign and malignant pulmonary nodules in lung cancer diagnosis. Itâ€™s commonly known that a premise of training complex deep neural nets is the large-scale labeled datasets. However, the abundance of labeled datasets is usually unavailable in many medical image domains. This factor can lead to the poor generalization performance of deep learning models. Thus, we proposed a novel multi-discriminator generative adversarial network model combined with an encoder using unsupervised method for the classification of benign and malignant pulmonary nodules.

Overview of MDGAN


An overview of the proposed classification approach of lung nodules is shown in this picture, which mainly includes three steps: (a) sample datasets construction; (b) model training: multi-discriminator generative adversarial network training and MDGAN guided encoder training; (c) lung nodule malignancy classification. Using the publicly available LIDC-IDRI dataset, information of lung nodule locations and their benign and malignant levels are extracted from the XML file and the ROI regions of lung nodules are segmented to form the sample dataset. The sample set is divided into a training set and a test set. Data argumentation was performed on the training set.

Unlabeled benign pulmonary nodules were used to train the multi-discriminator GAN network. The trained generator and discriminator were combined with an encoder with the real unmarked lung nodules image mapped to the latent space. The spatial data representation was subsequently used as an input to the MDGAN network generator. The corresponding image is generated based on the compressed data representation, making the difference between the real image and the generated image very small, and the degree of their deviation will be used to calculate the abnormal score. The higher the abnormal score is, the higher the degree of malignant lung nodules is. Benign and malignant classification results can then be obtained according to the defined threshold.
